About Darbuka

About Hadrah and Darbuka

Hadrah art is a musical art that is used as a medium of da'wah and worship of Allah SWT and the Prophet Muhammad SAW by people who adhere to Islamic beliefs, but has changed its form into a popular show and serves as entertainment.

Darbuka Musical Instrument is one of the musical instruments in Hadrah Arts where the Darbuka is shaped like a cormorant and made of aluminum with an average diameter of 8.75 inches or about 22 cm. Darbuka is played when the song enters the chorus by hitting it with a finger and making a dum, no, ka, and slap sound.

MORE ABOUT DARBUKA

The goblet drum (also chalice drum, tarabuka, tarabaki, darbuka, derbake, debuka, doumbek, dumbec, dumbeg, dumbelek, toumperleki, tumbak, or zerbaghali, (Egyptian Arabic: دربوكة / "Romanized": Darbuka) is a single head membranophone with a goblet shaped body used mostly in Egypt and is considered the National symbol of Egyptian Shaabi Music, also in parts of the West Asia, North Africa, South Asia, and Eastern Europe. Tone of Darbuka

Basic Tone of Darbuka

The basic tone of the darbuka musical instrument

Dum

Press the right hand flat and let it bounce after the blow, fingers in a tight and straight position.

Tak / Ka

Arrange the middle and ring fingers then press the edge of the mica.

Slap

The fingers should be opened more loosely than the dum and pressing on the mica darbuka.

Tone Pattern of Darbuka

The tone pattern of the darbuka musical instrument

Baladi

DUM - DUM - pass - TAK - pass - DUM - pass - TAK

Maqsum

DUM - TAK - pass - TAK - pass - DUM - TAK

Sayyidi

DUM - TAK - pass - DUM - DUM - pass - TAK

Darbuka Tone Identification
